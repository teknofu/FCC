Product Requirements Document (PRD)
Product Name: Family Chore Chart App

1. Introduction
The Family Chore Chart App is a web application designed to streamline the management and tracking of household chores. The app provides a centralized platform for parents to assign chores, children to mark chores as complete, and parents to approve and reward completed chores. By fostering accountability and teamwork, the app aims to make chore management easier and more engaging for families.

2. Goals and Objectives
Simplify chore management for parents by providing an intuitive platform for assigning and tracking chores.

Increase children's participation and accountability in household chores through a transparent and rewarding system.

Promote fairness in chore distribution and reward allocation.

Foster a sense of responsibility and teamwork within the family.

Engage children through gamification and a user-friendly interface.

3. Target Audience
The primary target audience is families in the United States with children aged 10 to 18. The app is designed to be accessible and engaging for both parents and children, with a focus on simplicity and ease of use.

4. User Roles and Permissions
The app will have two main user roles:

Parent
Create and manage user accounts for children.

Assign chores to children, including daily, weekly, and one-off chores.

Set the monetary value of each chore.

Approve or reject completed chores.

Track the chore completion progress of each child.

Manage and track earnings for each child.

Schedule chores, set reminders, and communicate with children through the app.

Child
View assigned chores.

Mark chores as complete.

Track their chore completion progress.

View their earnings.

Request changes to assigned chores.

5. Functional Requirements
User Authentication
Secure login system for both parent and child users.

Password reset functionality.

Chore Management
Create, edit, and delete chores.

Assign chores to specific children.

Categorize chores as daily, weekly, or one-off.

Set a monetary value for each chore.

Chore Completion Tracking
Children can mark chores as complete.

Parents can approve or reject completed chores.

Visual progress tracking for each child (e.g., progress bars, completion percentages).

Reward System
Track earnings for each child based on completed chores.

Parents can set a pay period (e.g., weekly, bi-weekly) for tracking earnings.

No integration with real-world payment systems (e.g., bank accounts, digital wallets). Parents will disperse funds outside the app.

Gamification Features
Badges: Awarded for completing a certain number of chores or reaching milestones.

Leaderboards: Display rankings among siblings (optional and customizable by parents).

Levels: Children can level up based on consistent chore completion.

Humor in UX: Incorporate lighthearted and engaging elements to reduce stress and keep children motivated.

Parent-Child Communication
Simple messaging system: Allow parents and children to communicate within the context of a chore (e.g., clarifying instructions or requesting changes).

Notifications and reminders: Send reminders for upcoming or overdue chores.

Advanced Analytics and Reporting
Provide parents with simple, easy-to-understand reports on chore completion trends, earnings over time, and individual child progress.

Reports should be visually intuitive (e.g., charts, graphs) and exportable for offline review.

6. User Interface (UI) and User Experience (UX) Requirements
Clean and intuitive design: The app should be easy to navigate for both parents and children.

Visually appealing for children: Use engaging colors, icons, and animations to make the app fun for children aged 10-18.

Mobile-first design: The app should be optimized for mobile devices, as this is the primary use case.

Responsive design: Ensure the app performs well on various devices and screen sizes.

7. Technical Requirements
The app will be developed as a web application accessible through a mobile browser.

Frontend: Built using Vite + React.

Backend: Powered by Firebase for authentication, database, and hosting.

Hosting: The app will be hosted on a reliable and scalable platform (e.g., Firebase Hosting).

Data Storage: User data (e.g., chore history, earnings) will be stored securely in password-protected profiles.

8. Success Metrics
Number of active users: Track the number of families using the app regularly.

Frequency of app usage: Measure how often parents and children interact with the app.

Engagement with gamification features: Monitor usage of badges, leaderboards, and levels.

User feedback and satisfaction: Collect feedback through in-app surveys or ratings.

9. Security and Privacy Requirements
The app will comply with COPPA (Children's Online Privacy Protection Act) and other applicable laws in the United States.

User data (e.g., chore history, earnings) will be stored securely in password-protected profiles.

No data will be shared with third parties or outside the app.

10. Accessibility and Localization
First iteration: The app will be available only in English.

Future iterations: Consider adding support for screen readers, colorblind-friendly designs, and multiple languages.

11. Other Considerations
Future Integrations
While not crucial for the first iteration, future versions of the app may integrate with:

Google Calendar: For syncing chore schedules and reminders.

Alexa and Google Home: For voice-based chore management.

Gamification Expansion
Consider adding non-monetary rewards (e.g., unlocking themes, avatars) to further engage children.

Maintenance and Updates
Plan for regular updates to fix bugs, improve performance, and add new features based on user feedback.

12. Conclusion
The Family Chore Chart App has the potential to significantly improve how families manage and track household chores. By providing a user-friendly platform that is accessible on mobile devices, the app empowers children to take ownership of their chores while giving parents the tools they need to manage household responsibilities effectively.

This PRD serves as a roadmap for the development team, ensuring that the final product meets the needs of families in the United States with children aged 10-18.
